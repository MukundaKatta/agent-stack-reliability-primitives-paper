This submission is an artifact-backed systems paper describing the agent-stack: six small, single-concern reliability libraries for LLM agents, distributed independently to npm, PyPI, and the Model Context Protocol registry. The paper does not claim model benchmark superiority and does not present new empirical results. It documents an artifact pattern: zero-dependency primitives, typed errors, automatic provider-shape adapters, and cross-language symmetry across TypeScript, Python, and MCP server implementations. The eighteen referenced packages are publicly available under the MIT license; source repositories live at github.com/MukundaKatta under the Agent* names. The paper situates the artifact in the design space of LLM agent reliability tooling, contrasting stackable primitives with monolithic frameworks, and identifies operational considerations and open questions for users who adopt the pattern.
